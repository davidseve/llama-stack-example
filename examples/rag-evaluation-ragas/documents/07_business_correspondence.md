**Subject:** Bridge 7 Rehabilitation - Preliminary Engineering Report and Construction Timeline

Dear City Manager Stevens,

Please find attached the preliminary engineering report for Bridge 7 rehabilitation project. This email summarizes key findings and recommended action items.

**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:**

Bridge 7, constructed in 1963, has reached critical structural deficiency requiring immediate intervention. Our inspection conducted October 5-10, 2024, identified multiple failure points that necessitate comprehensive rehabilitation rather than incremental repairs.

**STRUCTURAL DEFICIENCIES IDENTIFIED:**

1. **Primary Support System:**
   - Pylon displacement: 15.3 cm (6.0 inches) from design specifications
   - Load capacity reduced to 65% of original 40-ton design
   - Current safe working load: 25 tons (with 15-ton restriction implemented)
   - Micro-cracking observed in 8 of 12 primary support beams

2. **Foundation Integrity:**
   - Differential settlement detected in eastern foundation
   - Erosion of supporting soil matrix
   - Groundwater infiltration compromising concrete integrity

3. **Dynamic Response:**
   - Natural frequency reduced 18% from original design
   - Indicates structural fatigue and material degradation
   - Vibration analysis shows concerning resonance patterns

**FAILURE TIMELINE PROJECTION:**

Without intervention:
- 8-14 months to potential catastrophic failure
- Failure probability increases exponentially after 10-month mark
- Current 15-ton restriction must remain in place

**RECOMMENDED SOLUTION:**

Comprehensive rehabilitation including:
- Foundation reinforcement and stabilization
- Primary beam replacement (8 beams)
- Pylon realignment and anchoring
- Deck resurfacing and waterproofing
- Installation of structural monitoring system

**COST ESTIMATE:**

- Engineering and design: $180,000
- Construction: $1,890,000
- Inspection and testing: $125,000
- Project management: $145,000
- Contingency (10%): $234,000
- **TOTAL: $2,574,000**

Note: Council approved budget of $2,500,000 is slightly below our estimate. Recommend securing additional $75,000 contingency or accepting reduced contingency risk.

**TIMELINE:**

- Final design completion: November 15, 2024
- Bid advertisement: November 18-December 9, 2024
- Contractor selection: December 16, 2024
- Pre-construction mobilization: December 20-31, 2024
- Construction start: January 2, 2025
- Projected completion: July 15, 2025
- Testing and inspection: July 16-31, 2025
- **ANTICIPATED REOPENING: August 1, 2025**

**ALTERNATIVE ROUTING ANALYSIS:**

Our traffic engineering consultant (Martinez Transportation Group) completed routing analysis:
- Primary detour: Highway 45 to Oak Street Bridge
- Average additional distance: 3.2 miles
- Average additional time: 4.7 minutes (normal traffic)
- Peak hour additional time: 8-12 minutes
- Commercial vehicle additional time: 6-9 minutes
